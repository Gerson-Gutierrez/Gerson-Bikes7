TYPES OF BICYCLES

ROAD BIKE

Road bikes are one of the oldest types of bicycles, yet they have remained mostly unchanged over time, despite the use of cutting-edge technology. Frames are frequently made of lightweight aluminium or carbon, with an aggressive form that places the driver’s knees almost horizontally over the pedals.

They are characterised by their great tyres and drop handlebars, which enable the rider to maintain an aerodynamic position during high-speed races.

MOUNTAIN BIKE

Mountain biking has grown in popularity over the previous decade and is now arguably the most popular type of riding. Mountain biking currently comes in a variety of styles, each with its own individual bike design.

The most noticeable difference between mountain bikes and other bikes is the presence of suspension. MTBs are usually equipped with a hardtail fork or both front and rear suspension, and are known as mountain full suspension bikes.

FAT TIRE BIKE

Fat tyre bicycles have grown in popularity over the last decade, particularly in beach towns and winter destinations. Fatty mountain bikes, as the name indicates, are larger than standard pneumatic mountain bikes and are capable of traversing soft terrain such as snow and sand.

During the 1980s, when mountain bikes were popular, some adventurous drivers modified single-speed ballon pull cruisers to explore the wilderness, resulting in the first fat motorbikes.
